## Section 1 - [1~]

1. **Cell** - 细胞<br>The basic unit of all living organisms; it is surrounded by a cell surface membrane and contains genetic material (DNA) and cytoplasm containing organelles

2. **Eukaryote** - 真核生物<br>An organism whose cells contain a nucleus and other membrane-bound organelles

3. **Nucleus** - 细胞核<br>(plural nuclei) a relatively large organelle found in eukaryotic cells, but absent from prokaryotic cells; the nucleus contains the cell’s DNA and therefore controls the activities of the cell; it is surrounded by two membranes which together form the nuclear envelope

4. **Organelle** - 细胞器<br>A functionally and structurally distinct part of a cell,e.g. a ribosome or mitochondrion

5. **Prokaryote** - 原核生物<br>An organism whose cells do not contain a nucleus or any other membrane-bound organelles

6. **Cell surface membrane** - 细胞膜<br>A very thin membrane (about 7 nm diameter) surrounding all cells; it is partially permeable and controls the exchange of materials between the cell and its environment

7. **Cell wall** - 细胞壁<br>A wall surrounding prokaryote, plant and fungal cells; the wall contains a strengthening material which protects the cell from mechanical damage, supports it and prevents it from bursting by osmosis if the cell is surrounded by a solution with a higher water potential

8. **Chloroplast** - 叶绿体<br>The organelle, bounded by an envelope (i.e., two membranes), in which photosynthesis takes place in eukaryotes

9. **Chromatin** - 染色质<br>The material of which chromosomes are made, consisting of DNA, proteins and small amounts of RNA; visible as patches or fibres within the nucleus when stained

10. **Chromosome** - 染色体<br>In the nucleus of the cells of eukaryotes, a structure made of tightly coiled chromatin (DNA, proteins and RNA) visible during cell division; the term circular DNA is now commonly used for the circular strand of DNA present in a prokaryotic cell

11. **Cytoplasm** - 细胞质<br>The contents of a cell, excluding the nucleus

12. **Grana** - 基粒<br>(singular granum)  stacks of membranes inside a chloroplast

13. **Mitochondrion** - 线粒体<br>(plural mitochondria) the organelle in eukaryotes in which aerobic respiration takes place

14. **Nucleolus** - 核仁<br>A small structure, one or more of which is found inside the nucleus; the nucleolus is usually visible as a densely stained body; its function is to manufacture ribosomes using the information in its own DNA

15. **Photosynthesis** - 光合作用<br>The production of organic substances from inorganic ones, using energy from light

16. **Plasmodesma** - 胞间连丝<br>(plural plasmodesmata) a pore-like structure found in plant cell walls; plasmodesmata of neighbouring plant cells lineup to form tube-like pores through the cell walls, allowing the controlled passage of materials from one cell to the other; the pores contain ER and are lined with the cell surface membrane

17. **Protoplasm** - 原生质<br>All the living material inside a cell (cytoplasm plus nucleus)

18. **Tonoplast** - 液泡膜<br>The partially permeable membrane that surrounds plant vacuoles

19. **Vacuole** - 液泡<br>An organelle found in eukaryotic cells; alarge, permanent central vacuole is atypical feature of plant cells, where it has a variety of functions, including storage of biochemicals such as salts, sugars and waste products; temporary vacuoles, such as phagocytic vacuoles (also known as phagocytic vesicles),may form in animal cells; see endocytosis

20. **Eyepiece graticule** - 目镜刻尺<br>Small scale that is placed in a microscope eyepiece

## Section 2 - [21~]

21. **Magnification** - 放大倍数<br>The number of times larger animage of an object is than the real size of the object; magnification = image size ÷ actual (real) size of the object

22. **Micrograph** - 显微照片<br>A picture taken with the aid of a microscope; a photomicrograph (or light micrograph) is taken using alight microscope; an electron micrograph is taken using an electron microscope

23. **Stage micrometer** - 台尺<br>Very small, accurately drawn scale of known dimensions, engraved on a microscope slide

24. **Resolution** - 分辨率<br>The ability to distinguish between two objects very close together; the higher the resolution of an image, the greater the detail that can be seen

25. **ADP (adenosine diphosphate)** - 二磷酸腺苷 (ADP)<br>The molecule that is converted to ATP by addition of phosphate (a reaction known as phosphorylation) during cell respiration; the enzyme responsible is ATP synthase; the reaction requires energy

26. **ATP (adenosine triphosphate)** - 三磷酸腺苷 (ATP)<br>The molecule that is the universal energy currency in all living cells; the purpose of respiration is to make ATP

27. **Centriole** - 中心粒<br>One of two small, cylindrical structures, made from microtubules, found just outside the nucleus in animal cells, in a region known as the centrosome; they are also found at the bases of cilia and flagella

28. **Centrosome** - 中心体<br>The main microtubule organising centre (MTOC) in animal cells

29. **Cilia** - 纤毛<br>(singular cilium) whip-like structures projecting from the surface of many animal cells and the cells of many unicellular organisms; they beat, causing locomotion or the movement of fluid across the cell surface

30. **Cristae** - 嵴<br> (singular crista) folds of the inner membrane of the mitochondrial envelope on which are found stalked particles of ATP synthase and electron transport chains associated with aerobic respiration

31. **Endoplasmic reticulum (ER)** - 内质网 (ER)<br>A network of flattened sacs running through the cytoplasm of eukaryotic cells; molecules, particularly proteins, can be transported through the cell inside the sacs separate from the rest of the cytoplasm; ER is continuous with the outer membrane of the nuclear envelope

32. **Flagella** - 鞭毛<br> (singular flagellum) whip-like structures projecting from the surface of some animal cells and the cells of many unicellular organisms; they beat, causing locomotion or the movement of fluid across the cell surface; they are identical in structure to cilia, but longer 

33. **Golgi body (Golgi apparatus, Golgi complex)** - 高尔基体 (高尔基器，高尔基复合体)<br>An organelle found in eukaryotic cells; the Golgi apparatus consists of a stack of flattened sacs, constantly forming at one end and breaking up into Golgi vesicles at the other end

34. **Golgi vesicles** - 高尔基小泡<br>Carry their contents to other parts of the cell, often to the cell surface membrane for secretion; the Golgi apparatus chemically modifies the molecules it transports, e.g. sugars maybe added to proteins to make glycoproteins

35. **Lysosome** - 溶酶体<br>A spherical organelle found in eukaryotic cells; it contains digestive (hydrolytic) enzymes and has a variety of destructive functions, such as removal of old cell organelles

36. **Microtubules** - 微管<br>Tiny tubes made of a protein called tubulin and found in most eukaryotic cells; microtubules have a large variety of functions, including cell support and determining cell shape; the ‘spindle’ on which chromosomes separate during nuclear division is made of microtubules

37. **Microvilli** - 微绒毛<br>(singular microvillus) small, finger-like extensions of a cell which increase the surface area of the cell for more efficient absorption or secretion

38. **Nuclear envelope** - 核膜<br>The two membranes, situated close together, that surround the nucleus; the envelope is perforated with nuclear pores

39. **Nuclear pores** - 核孔<br>Pores found in the nuclear envelope which control the exchange of materials, e.g. mRNA, between the nucleus and the cytoplasm

40. **Ribosome** - 核糖体<br>A tiny organelle found in large numbers in all cells; prokaryotic ribosomes are smaller (20 nm diameter) than eukaryotic ribosomes (25 nm diameter)

## Section 3 - [41~]

41. **Thylakoid** - 类囊体<br>A flattened, membrane-bound, fluid-filled sac which is the site of the light-dependent reactions of photosynthesis in a chloroplast

42. **Bacteria** - 细菌<br> (singular bacterium) a group of single-celled prokaryotic microorganisms; they have a number of characteristics, such as the ability to form spores, which distinguish them from the other group of prokaryotes known as Archaea

43. **Peptidoglycan** - 肽聚糖<br>A polysaccharide combined with amino acids; it is also known as murein; it makes the bacterial cell wall more rigid

44. **Plasmid** - 质粒<br>A small circular piece of DNA in a bacterium (not its main ‘chromosome’); plasmids often contain genes that confer resistance to antibiotics

45. **Murein** - 粘肽<br>See peptidoglycan

46. **Phospholipid** - 磷脂<br>A lipid to which phosphate is added; the molecule is made up of a glycerol molecule, two fatty acids and a phosphate group; a double layer (a bilayer) of phospholipids forms the basic structure of all cell membranes

47. **Virus** - 病毒<br>Avery small (20–300 nm) infectious particle which can replicate only inside living cells; it consists of a molecule of DNA or RNA (the genome) surrounded by a protein coat; an outer lipid envelope may also be present 

48. **Condensation reaction** - 缩合反应<br>A chemical reaction involving the joining together of two molecules by removal of a water molecule

49. **Hydrolysis** - 水解<br>A chemical reaction in which a chemical bond is broken by the addition of a water molecule; commonly used to breakdown complex molecules into simpler molecules

50. **Macromolecule** - 大分子<br>See also polymer; a large molecule such as a polysaccharide, protein or nucleic acid

51. **Monomer** - 单体<br>A relatively simple molecule which is used as a basic building block for the synthesis of a polymer; many monomers are joined together to make the polymer, usually by condensation reactions; common examples of monomers are monosaccharides, amino acids and nucleotides

52. **Monosaccharide** - 单糖<br>A molecule consisting of a single sugar unit and with the general formula (CH2O)n

53. **Polymer** - 聚合物<br>A giant molecule made from many similar repeating subunits joined together in a chain; the subunits are much smaller and simpler molecules known as monomers; examples of biological polymers are polysaccharides, proteins and nucleic acids

54. **Benedict’s test** - 本尼迪克特试验<br>A test for the presence of reducing sugars; the unknown substance is heated with Benedict’s reagent, and a change from a clear blue solution to the production of a yellow, red or brown precipitate indicates the presence of reducing sugars such as glucose

55. **Cellulose** - 纤维素<br>A polysaccharide made from beta-glucose subunits; used as astrengthening material in plant cell walls 

56. **Disaccharide** - 双糖<br>A sugar molecule consisting of two monosaccharides joined together by a glycosidic bond

57. **Glycogen** - 糖原<br>A polysaccharide made of many glucose molecules linked together, that acts as a glucose store in liver and muscle cells

58. **Glycosidic bond** - 糖苷键<br>A C–O–C link between two sugar molecules, formed by a condensation reaction; it is a covalent bond

59. **Hydrogen bond** - 氢键<br>A relatively weak bond formed by the attraction between a group with a small positive charge on a hydrogen atom (H∂+) and another group carrying a small negative charge (∂–),e.g. between two –Oδ–Hδ+ groups

60. **Polysaccharide** - 多糖<br>A polymer whose subunits are monosaccharides joined together by glycosidic bonds

## Section 4 - [61~]

61. **Ester bond / ester linkage** - 酯键 / 酯键连接<br>A chemical bond, represented as –COO– , formed when an acid reacts with an alcohol 

62. **Triglyceride** - 三酰甘油<br>A type of lipid formed when three fatty acid molecules combine with glycerol, an alcohol with three hydroxyl (−OH) groups

63. **Biuret test** - 双缩脲试验<br>A test for the presence of amine groups and thus for the presence of protein; biuret reagent is added to the unknown substance, and a change from pale blue to purple indicates the presence of protein

64. **Collagen** - 胶原蛋白<br>The main structural protein of animals; known as ‘white fibres’, the fundamental unit of the fibre consists of three helical polypeptide chains wound around each other, forming a ‘triple helix’ with high tensile strength

65. **Fibrous protein** - 纤维蛋白<br>A protein whose molecules have a relatively long, thin structure that is generally insoluble and metabolically inactive, and whose function is usually structural,e.g. keratin and collagen

66. **Globular protein** - 球状蛋白<br>A protein whose molecules are folded into a relatively spherical shape, often has physiological roles and is often water-soluble and metabolically active, e.g. insulin, haemoglobin and enzymes

67. **Haemoglobin** - 血红蛋白<br>The red pigment found in red blood cells, whose molecules contain four iron atoms within a globular protein made up of four polypeptides; it combines reversibly with oxygen

68. **Peptide bond** - 肽键<br>A C–N link between two amino acid molecules, formed by a condensation reaction; the covalent bond joining neighbouring amino acids together in proteins

69. **Polypeptide** - 多肽<br>Along chain of amino acids formed by condensation reactions between the individual amino acids; proteins are made of one or more polypeptide chains; see peptide bond

70. **Primary structure** - 一级结构<br>The sequence of amino acids in a polypeptide or protein

71. **Quaternary structure** - 四级结构<br>The three-dimensional arrangement of two or more polypeptides, or of a polypeptide and anon-protein component such ashaem, in a protein molecule

72. **Secondary structure** - 二级结构<br>The structure of a protein molecule resulting from the regular coiling or folding of the chain of amino acids (an α-helix or β-pleated sheet)

73. **Sickle cell anaemia** - 镰状细胞贫血<br>A genetic disease caused by a faulty gene coding for haemoglobin, in which haemoglobin tends to precipitate when oxygen concentrations are low

74. **Tertiary structure** - 三级结构<br>The compact structure of a protein molecule resulting from the three-dimensional coiling of the already-folded chain of amino acids

75. **Α-helix** - α-螺旋<br>A helical structure formed by a polypeptide chain,held in place by hydrogen bonds; an α-helix is an example of secondary structure in a protein

76. **Β-pleated sheet** - β-折叠<br>A loose, sheet-like structure formed by hydrogen bonding between parallel polypeptide chains; a β-pleated sheet is an example of secondary structure in a protein

## Section 5 - [77~]

77. **Enzyme** - 酶<br>A protein produced by a living organism that acts as a catalyst in a specific reaction by reducing activation energy

78. **Activation energy** - 活化能<br>The energy that must be provided to make a reaction take place; enzymes reduce the activation energy required for a substrate to change into a product

79. **Active site** - 活性部位<br>An area on an enzyme molecule where the substrate can bind

80. **Induced-fit hypothesis** - 诱导契合假说<br>A hypothesis for enzyme action; the substrate is a complementary shape to the active site of the enzyme, but not an exact fit – the enzyme or sometimes the substrate can change shape slightly to ensure a perfect fit, but it is still described as showing specificity

81. **Lock-and-key hypothesis** - 锁钥假说<br>A hypothesis for enzyme action; the substrate is a complementary shape to the active site of the enzyme, and fits exactly into the site; the enzyme shows specificity for the substrate

82. **Colorimeter** - 比色计<br>An instrument that measures the colour of a solution by measuring the absorption of different wavelengths of light

83. **Vmax** - 最大速度 (Vmax)<br>The theoretical maximum rate of an enzyme-controlled reaction, obtained when all the active sites of the enzyme are occupied

84. **Michaelis–Menten constant (Km)** - 米氏常数 (Km)<br>The substrate concentration at which an enzyme works at half its maximum rate (½Vmax), used as a measure of the efficiency of an enzyme; the lower the value of Km, the more efficient the enzyme

85. **Km** - 米氏常数 (Km)<br>See Michaelis–Menten constant

86. **Competitive inhibition** - 竞争性抑制<br>When a substance reduces the rate of activity of an enzyme by competing with the substrate molecules for the enzyme’s active site; increasing substrate concentration reduces the degree of inhibition; increasing inhibitor concentration increases the degree of inhibition

87. **Non-competitive inhibition** - 非竞争性抑制<br>When a substance reduces the rate of activity of an enzyme, but increasing the concentration of the substrate does not reduce the degree of inhibition; many non-competitive inhibitors bind to areas of the enzyme molecule other than the active site itself

88. **Immobilised enzymes** - 固定化酶<br>Enzymes that have been fixed to a surface or trapped inside beads of agar gel

## Section 6 - [89~]

89. **Fluid mosaic model** - 流动镶嵌模型<br>The currently accepted model of membrane structure, proposed by Singer and Nicolson in 1972, in which protein molecules are free to move about in a fluid bilayer of phospholipid molecules

90. **Cholesterol** - 胆固醇<br>A small,lipid-related molecule with a hydrophilic head and a hydrophobic tail which is an essential constituent of membranes; it is particularly common in animal cells and gives flexibility and stability to the membrane as well as reducing fluidity

91. **Cell signalling** - 细胞信号传导<br>The molecular mechanisms by which cells detect and respond to external stimuli, including communication between cells

92. **Ligand** - 配体<br>A biological molecule which binds specifically to another molecule, such as a cell surface membrane receptor, during cell signalling

93. **Transduction** - 转导<br>Occurs during cell signalling and is the process of converting a signal from one method of transmission to another

94. **Active transport** - 主动运输<br>The movement of molecules orions through transport proteins across a cell membrane, against their concentration gradient, using energy from ATP

95. **Carrier protein** - 载体蛋白<br>A membrane protein which changes shape to allow the passage into or out of the cell of specific ions or molecules by facilitated diffusion or active transport

96. **Channel protein** - 通道蛋白<br>A membrane protein of fixed shape which has a water-filled pore through which selected hydrophilic ions or molecules can pass; see facilitated diffusion

97. **Diffusion** - 扩散<br>The net movement of molecules orions from a region of higher concentration to a region of lower concentration down a concentration gradient, as a result of the random movements of particles

98. **Endocytosis** - 内吞作用<br>The bulk movement of liquids (pinocytosis) or solids (phagocytosis) into a cell, by the infolding of the cell surface membrane to form vesicles containing the substance; endocytosis is an active process requiring ATP

99. **Exocytosis** - 外排作用<br>The bulk movement of liquids or solids out of a cell, by the fusion of vesicles containing the substance with the cell surface membrane; exocytosis is an active process requiring ATP

100. **Facilitated diffusion** - 协助扩散<br>The diffusion of a substance through atransport protein (channel protein or carrier protein) in a cell membrane; the protein provides hydrophilic areas that allow the molecule orion to pass through the membrane, which would otherwise be less permeable to it 

101. **Incipient plasmolysis** - 初现质壁分离<br>The point at which plasmolysis is about to occur when a plant cell or a prokaryote cell is losing water; at this point the protoplast is exerting no pressure on the cell wall

102. **Osmosis** - 渗透作用<br>The net diffusion of water molecules from a region of higher water potential to a region of lower water potential, through a partially permeable membrane 

103. **Phagocyte** - 吞噬细胞<br>A type of cell that ingests (eats) and destroys pathogens or damaged body cells by the process of phagocytosis; some phagocytes are white blood cells.

104. **Plasmolysis** - 质壁分离<br>The loss of water from a plant or prokaryote cell to the point where the protoplast shrinks away from the cell wall

105. **Protoplast** - 原生质体<br>The living contents of a plant cell, including the cell surface membrane but excluding the cell wall

106. **Sodium–potassium pump (Na+–K+ pump)** - 钠钾泵 (Na+–K+ 泵)<br>A membrane protein (or proteins) that moves sodium ions out of a cell and potassium ions into it, using ATP

107. **Water potential** - 水势<br>A measure of the tendency of water to move from one place to another; water moves from a solution with higher water potential to one with lower water potential; water potential is decreased by the addition of solute, and increased by the application of pressure; symbol for water potential is Ψ or Ψw

108. **Phagocytosis** - 吞噬作用<br>See endocytosis

109. **Pinocytosis** - 胞饮作用<br>See endocytosis

## Section 7 - [110~]

110. **Chromatid** - 染色单体<br>One of two identical parts of a chromosome, held together by a centromere, formed during interphase by the replication of the DNA strand

111. **Cell cycle** - 细胞周期<br>The sequence of events that takes place from one cell division until the next; it is made up of interphase, mitosis and cytokinesis

112. **Mitosis** - 有丝分裂<br>The division of a nucleus into two so that the two daughter cells have exactly the same number and type of chromosomes as the parent cell

113. **Asexual reproduction** - 无性繁殖<br>The production of new individuals of a species by a single parent organism

114. **Kinetochore** - 着丝粒<br>A protein structure found at the centromere of a chromatid to which microtubules attach during nuclear division

115. **Stem cell** - 干细胞<br>A relatively unspecialised cell that retains the ability to divide an unlimited number of times, and which has the potential to become a specialised cell (such as a blood cell or muscle cell)

116. **Telomere** - 端粒<br>Repetitive sequence of DNA at the end of a chromosome that protects genes from the chromosome shortening that happens at each cell division

117. **Cancers** - 癌症<br>A group of diseases that result from a breakdown in the usual control mechanisms that regulate cell division; certain cells divide uncontrollably and form tumours, from which cells may breakaway and form secondary tumours in other areas of the body (metastasis)

118. **Carcinogen** - 致癌物<br>A substance or environmental factor that can cause cancer

119. **Mutation** - 突变<br>A random change in the base sequence (structure) of DNA (a gene mutation), or in the structure and/or number of chromosomes (achromosome mutation)

## Section 8 - [120~]

120. **Complementary base pairing** - 互补碱基配对<br>The hydrogen bonding of A with T or U and of C with G in nucleic acids

121. **Dinucleotide** - 二核苷酸<br>Two nucleotides joined together by a phosphodiester bond

122. **Nucleotide** - 核苷酸<br>A molecule consisting of a nitrogen-containing base, a pentose sugar and a phosphate group

123. **Phosphodiester bond** - 磷酸二酯键<br>A bond joining two nucleotides together; there are two ester bonds, one from the shared phosphate group to each of the sugars either side of it

124. **Polynucleotide** - 多核苷酸<br>A chain of nucleotides joined together by phosphodiester bonds

125. **DNA ligase** - DNA连接酶<br>An enzyme that catalyses the joining together of two nucleotides with covalent phosphodiester bonds during DNA replication; used when inserting DNA into a plasmid

126. **DNA polymerase** - DNA聚合酶<br>An enzyme that copies DNA; it runs along the separated DNA strands lining up one complementary nucleotide at a time ready for joining by DNA ligase

127. **Gene** - 基因<br>A length of DNA that codes for a particular protein or polypeptide

128. **Lagging strand** - 后随链<br>During DNA replication, the parent strand that runs in the 5′ to 3′ direction is copied to produce the lagging strand

129. **Leading strand** - 领先链<br>During DNA replication, the parent strand that runs in the 3′ to 5′ direction is copied to produce the leading strand

130. **Semi-conservative replication** - 半保留复制<br>The method by which a DNA molecule is copied to form two identical molecules, each containing one strand from the original molecule and one newly synthesised strand

131. **Transcription** - 转录<br>Copying the genetic information in a molecule of DNA into a complementary strand of mRNA; a single strand of the DNA is used as a template (this is called the template or transcribed strand) – the enzyme responsible is RNA polymerase

132. **Translation** - 翻译<br>A stage in protein synthesis during which a sequence of nucleotides in a molecule of messenger RNA (mRNA) is converted (translated) into a corresponding sequence of amino acids in a polypeptide chain; it takes place at ribosomes

133. **Anticodon** - 反密码子<br>Sequence of three unpaired bases on a tRNA molecule that binds with a codon on mRNA

134. **Chromosome mutation** - 染色体突变<br>A random and unpredictable change in the structure or number of chromosomes in a cell

135. **Codon** - 密码子<br>Sequence of three bases on an mRNA molecule that codes for a specific amino acid or for a stop signal

136. **Gene mutation** - 基因突变<br>A change in the base sequence in part of a DNA molecule

137. **Frame-shift mutation** - 移码突变<br>A type of gene mutation causedby insertion or deletion of one or more nucleotides, resulting in incorrect reading of the sequence of triplets in the genetic code due to a shift in the reading frame

## Section 9 - [138~]

138. **Vascular** - 维管<br>A term referring to tubes or vessels (from the Latin ‘vascul’,meaning vessel)

139. **Vascular system** - 维管系统<br>A system of fluid-filled tubes, vessels or spaces, most commonly used for long-distance transport in living organisms; examples are the blood vascular system in animals and the vascular system of xylem and phloem in plants

140. **Collenchyma** - 厚角组织<br>A modified form of parenchymain which the corners of the cells have extra cellulose thickening, providing extra support, as in the midrib of leaves and at the corners of square stems; in three dimensions the tissue occurs in strands (as in celery petioles)

141. **Dicotyledon** - 双子叶植物<br>Flowering plants can be classified as monocotyledons or dicotyledons; the seeds of dicotyledonous plants contain an embryo with two cotyledons (seed leaves) in their seeds and the adult plant typically has leaves with a blade (lamina) and astalk (petiole)

142. **Endodermis** - 内皮层<br>The layer of cells surrounding the vascular tissue of plants; it is most clearly visible in roots

143. **Epidermis** - 表皮<br>The outer layer of cells covering the body of a plant or animal; in plants it is usually one cell thick and maybe covered with a cuticle which provides additional protection against loss of water and disease

144. **Lignin** - 木质素<br>A hard material made by plants and used to strengthen the cell walls of certain types of cell, particularly xylem vessel elements and sclerenchyma cells; it is the main material in wood

145. **Parenchyma** - 薄壁组织<br>A basic plant tissue typically used as packing tissue between more specialised structures; it is metabolically active and may have a variety of functions such as food storage and support; parenchyma cells also play an important role in the movement of water and food products in the xylem and phloem

146. **Phloem** - 韧皮部<br>A tissue containing tubes called sieve tubes and other types of cell, responsible for the transport through the plant of organic solutes (assimilates) such as sucrose

147. **Sclerenchyma** - 厚壁组织<br>A plant tissue consisting of thick-walled cells with a purely mechanical function (strength and support); the cell walls have usually become impregnated with lignin and the mature cells are dead with no visible contents; many sclerenchyma cells take the form of fibres

148. **Stage micrometer** - 台尺<br>Very small, accurately drawn scale of known dimensions, engraved on a microscope slide

149. **Vascular bundle** - 维管束<br>A strand of vascular tissue running longitudinally in a plant; within the bundle, the arrangement of tissues like xylem, phloem and sclerenchyma varies in different plants and organs

150. **Vascular tissue** - 维管组织<br>A tissue in plants consisting mainly of xylem and phloem but also containing sclerenchyma and parenchyma cells

151. **Xylem** - 木质部<br>A tissue containing tubes called vessels and other types of cell, responsible for the transport of water through a plant and for support

152. **Eyepiece graticule** - 目镜刻尺<br>Small scale that is placed in a microscope eyepiece

153. **Apoplast pathway** - 质外体途径<br>The non-living system of interconnected cell walls extending throughout a plant, used as a transport pathway for the movement of water and mineral ions

154. **Cuticle** - 角质层<br>A layer covering, and secreted by, the epidermis; in plants it is made of a fatty substance called cutin, which helps to provide protection against water loss and infection

155. **Mesophyll** - 叶肉<br>The region of a leaf between the upper and lower epidermis; in dicotyledonous plants the mesophyll has an upper palisade layer and a lower mesophyll layer; the palisade mesophyll cells are column-shaped and form the main photosynthetic layer, whereas the spongy mesophyll has large air spaces between the cells for gas exchange

156. **Stoma** - 气孔<br>(plural stomata) a pore in the epidermis of a leaf, bounded by two guard cells and needed for efficient gas exchange

157. **Symplast pathway** - 共质体途径<br>The living system of interconnected protoplasts extending throughout a plant, used as a transport pathway for the movement of water and solutes; individual protoplasts are connected via plasmodesmata

## Section 10 - [158~]
